Cats and Dogs

Cats and Dogs was my first Kaggle contest which I took as a practical way of consolidating the Machine Learning course I had just finished. I overreached myself, and I mean hugely, but it was an enjoyable failure

The contest was to write an algorithm that could tell whether a picture was of a cat or a dog.

Simple.

Not so much. Working with images is tough work and required a whole bunch of skills to be learnt before any of my new machine-learning knowledge could be applied.

My final product was an 86% accurate neural network but for a subproblem along the way.

  • I came at this problem with a two step approach. First I would train an algorithm that would, effectively, crop an image to the part that contained an animal. Second I would use the cropped image to work out the kind of animal.

    Image processing required use of the open source OpenCV computer vision library. This in turn meant resurrecting my rusty Java. To do the hard sums I used the nnet package from R.

    This produced some good results for the first part of my plan. Good results but slow. What did for me in the end was processing time.
